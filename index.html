general information

Instant noodles originated in Japan in the year 1958, made under the name Chikin Ramen, which had the goal to help end hunger in the country during that period.
The inventor knew it had to be delicious, nonperishable, easy to prepare, and inexpensive. That food was this 29-cent pack of instant ramen.
Today, 290 million people eat instant noodles, making it a common item you'll find in most households.
The Chinese term for this is 方便面 (fāng biàn miàn)-- convenient noodles, which is basically what it is: an easy, quick and cheap way to keep yourself from starving.
